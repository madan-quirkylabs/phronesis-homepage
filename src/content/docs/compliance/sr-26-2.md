# Federal Reserve SR 26-2: Model Risk Management for AI

The Federal Reserve's **SR 26-2** supervisory letter (2026) establishes parallel risk management expectations for Generative and Agentic AI systems used in banking — explicitly separating them from traditional Model Risk Management (MRM) frameworks.

<Aside type="caution" title="This is not legal advice">
This documentation reflects Phronesis's technical interpretation of SR 26-2 for product design purposes. Consult your Chief Risk Officer and legal team to determine specific applicability to your institution.
</Aside>

## Why SR 26-2 Matters for AI Deployments

SR 26-2 explicitly acknowledges that standard MRM frameworks (established under SR 11-7) are insufficient for governing generative and agentic AI because:

1. **Non-determinism**: Traditional models produce predictable outputs for given inputs. Generative AI produces probabilistic outputs that cannot be pre-validated.
2. **Emergent behavior**: Agentic AI systems adapt their actions in context — they cannot be governed using static input/output validation rules.
3. **Tool use and external state**: Agentic systems interact with external APIs, databases, and environments — dramatically expanding the blast radius of misbehavior.

SR 26-2 responds by requiring institutions to implement **novel governance frameworks** that account for runtime behavior, not just model validation.

## Key SR 26-2 Requirements

### "Effective Challenge" (Section III.B)

The most operationally challenging requirement. SR 26-2 mandates that institutions maintain the ability to provide **Effective Challenge** — meaning auditors and validators must be able to interrogate *why* a specific AI decision was made.

For traditional models, this meant examining model documentation and validation reports. For agentic AI, it means providing a **real-time, runtime trace** of the agent's decision sequence.

**How Phronesis satisfies this**: The [Forensic Reasoning Replay](/phronesis-homepage/docs/architecture/forensic-reasoning-replay) generates an Effective Challenge-ready timeline for every agent workflow, linking tool calls, LLM inferences, and data retrievals into a cohesive, auditable narrative.

### System of Record (Section IV.A)

SR 26-2 requires institutions to maintain a **System of Record** for AI agent deployments that documents:

- The agent's identity and version
- The workflows the agent is authorized to execute
- The runtime decisions made by the agent
- Any anomalies or deviations from expected behavior

**How Phronesis satisfies this**: The [Append-Only Ledger](/phronesis-homepage/docs/architecture/append-only-ledger) serves as the System of Record for all governed agent workflows. It is immutable, hash-chained, and automatically populated from OTel ingestion.

### Governance Escalation (Section III.D)

SR 26-2 requires institutions to define escalation paths when an agentic AI system exhibits unexpected behavior. The guidance specifies that escalation triggers should be automated, not dependent on manual log review.

**How Phronesis satisfies this**: The anomaly detection layer in the Forensic Reasoning Replay automatically flags policy violations and unexpected behaviors, triggering immediate alerts to the CRO dashboard without requiring engineering intervention.

### Data Lineage & Provenance (Section V.B)

SR 26-2 requires institutions to track which data sources informed each agent decision — particularly for decisions involving regulated information (e.g., sanctions lists, credit data, KYC records).

**How Phronesis satisfies this**: The Forensic Replay captures retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) source metadata, including document identity, version, and data freshness — flagging staleness that could compromise the integrity of a compliance decision.
